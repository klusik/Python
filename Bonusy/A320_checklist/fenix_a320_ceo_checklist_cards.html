How engine selection works Select CFM or IAE below. Shared items remain visible. Engine-specific items, tags and notes will switch automatically.
How abnormal and emergency cards are intended to be used In Fenix, ECAM remains the primary source of truth for active failures. These cards are quick action prompts and memory-style handling aids for flight use.
How state saving works Your progress is saved inside the browser on the current device. It persists when you close and reopen the page, unless the browser clears site data.

Notes

  • Normal checklists are laid out for practical Fenix use and quick scanning during flight phases.
  • Supplementary cards include common sim-relevant operational variants such as single-engine taxi, de-icing, low visibility, non-standard starts and return-to-gate style actions.
  • Abnormal and emergency cards prioritize quick cockpit handling prompts. They are not intended to replace ECAM logic or airline-specific QRH pages.
  • Engine differences are applied where they are operationally relevant for Fenix cockpit use, especially on start, restart and engine-specific notes.
  • This page is for simulation use. Always follow Fenix aircraft indications, ECAM, Fenix EFB performance tools and your chosen SOP where applicable.
